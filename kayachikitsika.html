Department of Kayachikitsa

Introduction

Ayurveda is the holistic approach, which is mainly concerned with the treatment of the body. In the process the person undergoes a change in the lifestyle, which includes change in food clothing and sometimes even residence to kayachikitsa. It is the first branch of Ashtang ayurveda or eight branched ayurveda that deals with general medicine. The total treatment procedure is called kaya chikitasa (Internal medicine) wherein kaya means Agni and chikitsa means treatment the entire ayurvedic therapeutics is based on the concept of agni. The concept of kaya agni is unique and is responsible for bio transformation. As it is known that energy can neither be created nor can it be destroyed in human body kaya provides the necessary energy for all bodily activities. The energy provided is derived from the food we eat and the air we breathe. The biological system of the body transforms this energy to one, which is utilized by the cells. Kayachikitsa is the one of the eight branches of ayurveda which deals with the multi systemic illness of the adults it deals with therapeutic approach to the disease either through shodhana (Purification) , Shamana (Pacification) and satwawajaya (Counseling), These applications are done through medications, behavioural psychological & life style management pharmacological intervention is through various forms of medicines like vati tablets, kashya decoctions, churna powders bhasma asava arista ghruta guggulus etc. strength of the system is providing efficient cost effective personalized medicine with high safety margins another unique aspect of kayachiktsa is enhances the sexual vigour and useful to procreate an ideal progeny. Department of kayachikitsa is backbone of clinical teaching and hospital care in every ayurvedic college.

Objectives

  • Deals with fundamentals of treatment of diseases.
  • Learning the clinical skill for diagnosis and treatment modalities as per principles of Ayurveda.
  • Students have to attend outdoor patient department and indoor patient department for practical learning.
  • To produce globally competitive Ayurved practitioners.
  • To become centre of excellence by providing quality education and practical training of Ayurved medicine.
  • Health for all.
  • Upholding the authentic principles of Ayurveda in order propagate health of well being world wide.
  • Dispensing of medicine as per individual patients.
  • Principles and practice of different regimens with follow ups.

Infrastructure

Highly educated dedicated clinical teaching staff. Department is well established with a class apart teaching faculty and having computer with internet facility. Department library with a good collection of ayurvedic and modern books is available throughout the academic hours. Well equipped well run OPD with adequate clinical facilities serving more the 18225 patients per year. In IPD 4217 bed occupying an average strength of more than 40 percentage of total bed strength providing service to more than 42 patients per year in IPD.

OPD Facility

Department conducts daily OPD at room No. 1& No. 2 in OPD block of the govt. ayurveda college hospital in college attached hospital rao OPD no. 1 for kayachikitsa department. General OPD deals with diseases from the following areas: Vata roga (Neurology), Sandhigat rog (Rheumatology including joint disorders), Tavacha rog (dermatology & veneral diseases), Udar Rog (Gastroenterology), Shavas Rog (Respiratory ailments), Hriaday rogs (Preventive cardiology), Aahar vihar janit rog (Life style disorders), Manas rog (Psychiatry), Vardha Awasth rog (Geriatrics).

IPD Facilities

Patient on the recommendation of the consultant approaches the IPD reception/ registration counter and after some basic formalities the patient is shifted to the admitted ward on allocated bed number. Separate male and female wards are available in new hospital private room facility start within few days. All the available laboratory investigations and Panchkarma procedures are free of charge to all the admitted patients. Patients admitted in the hospital are served the diet free of cost as prescribed by the consultant and under pathya system of the hospital.
